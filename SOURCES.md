## Research Note

This bibliography represents scholarly and cultural documentation of Hantu Tetek within Malaysian folklore. Sources range from colonial-era ethnographic documentation to contemporary anthropological analysis. Researchers should approach with cultural sensitivity and awareness that:

1. **Colonial Sources**: Early documentation reflects colonial perspectives and may contain biases
2. **Religious Context**: Islamic framework shapes modern interpretation
3. **Living Tradition**: This represents ongoing belief system for some communities
4. **Language**: Many primary sources in Malay language; translations may lose nuance
5. **Oral Tradition**: Written sources capture only portion of oral transmission

**Acknowledgment**: This documentation draws on work of numerous scholars, traditional knowledge keepers, and community members who have preserved and studied Malaysian folklore. We acknowledge the cultural custodianship of Malay communities and indigenous peoples whose traditions these sources document.
